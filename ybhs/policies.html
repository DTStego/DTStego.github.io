Paris Accords

The U.S. left the Paris Accords on November 4th, 2020.
Democratic presidential candidate Joe Biden vowed to
rejoin the Paris Accords if he becomes president.

An alliance between nearly all countries, the Accords aim to
limit the rise of greenhouse gases and end climate change.

Aims to prevent a 2°C temperature increase by reducing
carbon emissions, enlarging the usage of renewable energy,
and increasing energy efficiency.

Congressional Support

In 2016, Congress approved $7 billion in subsidies for
renewable energy. It funded jobs, massively decreased
production costs, and made the U.S. less reliant on fossil fuels.

Congress provided upwards of $4 million for the National
Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration, in 2019, to
conduct geoengineering monitoring & research efforts.
NOAA's Earth System Research Laboratory will research
and “examine the science behind geoengineering.”

Growth & Testing

The consumption of coal in the U.S. has decreased about 50%
while renewable energy sources increased about 48% from
2007 to 2019. Renewable energy overcame coal power for the
first time in a hundred years in 2019.

With funding of upwards of $20 million, Harvard University will
be one of the first to test & research geoengineering solutions,
more specifically aerosol injection. An attached regulatory
committee will oversee any health and environmental risks.
