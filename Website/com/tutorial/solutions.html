Giant reflector(s) that orbit Earth, blocking a portion of the Sun’s solar radiation.

Orbits around Earth’s Lagrange point, like a Goldilocks Zone, where mirrors have little risk of erroneously crashing to Earth.

Part of a few solar geoengineering solutions that block harmful rays & immense heat from ever reaching Earth.

Solar Energy

Uses large solar panels to absorb sunlight which is then converted into electricity.

Solar panels consist of solar cells that are commonly made of silicon due to silicon’s ability to absorb sunlight.

Due to its reliance on light from the Sun, solar panels don’t generate any electricity at night.

Carbon Capture

Through the process of Direct Air Capture (DAC), machines suck in CO2-filled air, filtering it.

Dirty filters can then be permanently stored in geological formations to prevent their harmful effects in the atmosphere.

DAC plants can fully run on clean energy, minimizing any environmental impact!

Wind Turbines

When wind blows past a turbine, blades capture its kinetic energy and rotate, creating usable, mechanical energy, recovering production costs.

Turbines can cause soil erosion, wildlife deaths due to collision, and sound & visual impact.

Producing a significant amount of wind energy requires a wind farm, which can take a long time due to planning, permits, and licenses.

Cost: 

Installation Time: About 2 - 8 years/farm

Environmental Impact:


Aerosol Injection

Like a volcanic eruption, but only less dangerous, aerosols are put into the stratosphere which then reflect light, and therefore heat, back into space.

Unstudied risk of ozone depletion, unknown weather effects, and other ethical issues.

Shown to be extremely effective in combating climate change and reducing temperatures, preventing many global warming consequences.

Geothermal Energy

Harnessing the massive amount of heat in the Earth, water is pumped deep into the ground, converted into steam when it cycles back up.

Hot steam can be vital to both electricity (through spinning a turbine) or for heating purposes, saving a massive amount of electricity.

Can cause foul odors, soil erosion, etc, but is very valuable, providing some countries most of their heating & electric demands.
